\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We described MARS, a single-binary terminal editor, multiplexer, and agent with memory
over the user's own context; plain lexical retrieval nearly closed the personalization
gap and substantially narrowed the self-knowledge gap. The through-line of both
system and agenda is ownership: local, private, compounding memory. A cloud
assistant starts every conversation a stranger; an agent that keeps its user's memory
becomes a little less generic and a little less amnesiac with every correction. The
same substrate, enriched, points to agents that learn your skills and understand your
work.
